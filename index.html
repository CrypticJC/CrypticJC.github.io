Get Into My Brain...

Solutions are available to every problem; we just have to come up with creative ways to reach them. I find it extraordinary to observe the digital revolution as it sculpts our society on a very personal level, from the cars that we drive to the accessories we wear. We are interlinking ourselves not only locally, but developing an ever more global community, a mass evolution. And the culprit? The Internet: A global network that’s getting more expansive day-by-day. A global network we rely on to complete daily tasks. A global network that has changed the way we as a race behave. We no longer remember volumes of information the same way, we need only remember where to find them, for an amass of data is just a finger tap away. It is this evolution I want to be a part of. As we entwine ourselves into our digital lives – our privacy suffers. We have become omnipresent in each other lives. We trust cooperation’s with our data, our digital lives, most of the time without a second thought.

Edinburgh Napier University

I'm advancing through a degree in Cyber Security & Forensics, deep diving into our new digital world where life without an email address is almost impossible to navigate. This course is the first in the UK to achieve full accreditation from the National Cyber Security Centre (part of GCHQ).

With less than 40-years as a university under its belt, Napier University has nearly 20,000 students in over 140 Countries and is within the top 10 UK universities for Computer Science.

A key focus of the lab is to create world-leading knowledge and innovation around citizen-focused systems which enshrine our right to privacy.
